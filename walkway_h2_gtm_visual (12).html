Customer success motion
Key distinction: There are two separate clocks. Onboarding starts when the contract is signed and lasts up to 10 days — this is technical setup. The CS motion starts the moment the first product goes live. Data, calls, and success metrics are all measured from go-live, not from the sign date.

Key principles (from LT meeting, June 8)
PrincipleDetail
Track contracted MRR separately from live MRRSigned ≠ WAAP. Report both: contracted MRR (what's signed) and active MRR (what's live on autopilot). The gap between the two is the onboarding backlog.
WAAP is the north star metricWeekly Active Autopilot Products — captures activation, retention, and expansion in a single number. All sales + CS activity should trace back to moving this number.
Comp on net new MRR, not % thresholdsCommission structure based on net new MRR generated. Include minimum product threshold per deal to prevent cherry-picking tiny accounts.
Handoff at Contract Signed, not Closed WonSales hands to CS at Contract Signed stage. CS owns onboarding → go live → motion. Sales pipeline closes at Contract Signed.
