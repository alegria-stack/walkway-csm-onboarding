Bokun · Ventrata · Xola propagation
Patterns across the last 90 days — many of these have been resolved but recur in different accounts or forms. They tell us where the system needs stronger foundations to scale reliably. Some may already be in progress.
Address first
Autopilot failure detection — silent delivery failures across booking systems
The problem
A product on autopilot can silently stop pushing prices to the booking system. There is no alert when this happens — discovery is reactive, either through manual checks or when an operator flags it.
Impact across accounts~8 tickets
Mi Bike Tours (Ventrata): 30 days, zero pushes, discovered by the operator. Pattern confirmed across Xola (Everyday California stalled), Bokun (Ciao Florence not picked up), and Ventrata again today (ENG-958). Venice Tours: direct-website bookings collapsed to ~0 under dynamic pricing — In Validation, Urgent.
The unlockFoundation exists
Any autopilot product on a monitoring list — absence or anomaly triggers an alert. Go-live window, movement tracking, and weekly digest cover the full picture. Emmanuel confirmed the foundation is ready to activate.
Trust & reliability
HighPricing accuracy — wrong currency, floor/ceiling behavior, pushes operators don't trust
The problem
Operators receive price outputs that don't match their expectations — wrong currency, stuck at ceiling, mass reverts, or pushes below the floor they set. Each incident prompts a check-in and erodes confidence in the algorithm at exactly the moment we need it strongest.
Impact across accounts~10 tickets
Amigo Mexico: USD/MXN currency mismatch blocking remaining Mexico go-live (ENG-963, Urgent, today). Carrani: price rules error blocking go-live. Airboat: algorithm at ceiling 4+ days. Everyday California: mass revert, prices too high. FX pipeline in validation (ENG-952).
The unlock
Better guardrails per booking system + FX pipeline in validation (ENG-952) resolves currency issues. Pricing accuracy and go-live monitoring work hand in hand — catching issues in the first 24 hours protects the Day 30 expansion conversation.
HighAutopilot movement tracking — ON→OFF→ON history not captured
The problem
When an operator turns autopilot off and back on, no timestamp or duration is captured. WAAP counts can be overstated, and Day 30 lift data is unreliable if autopilot was partially off during the measurement window.
Impact across accounts1 confirmed · pattern
Venice City Tours (Bokun): turned off autopilot on 3 products — not detected until team checked manually. False "Changes Waiting" status on autopilot products (Planned). Day 30 expansion conversation weakened because lift data looked inconsistent.
The unlock
Full movement log per product — WAAP is a reliable number and Day 30 lift data is trustworthy. For Scale accounts with 20–40 products, a silent drop on 5 products changes the expansion narrative before we even know it happened.
Scale & CS capacity
HighNo 30-day at-risk alert — accounts approaching Day 30 with low autopilot undetected
The problem
No automated flag exists when an account approaches Day 30 with WAAP below 90% threshold. This is tracked manually today and breaks at a specific account volume — not gradually.
Impact across accounts13 accounts · manual today
No confirmed missed cases at 13 accounts — manageable manually today. A missed Day 30 = missed expansion proposal = 4-week delay per account. At 35 accounts, multiple will hit Day 30 under threshold undetected.
The unlock
Automated flag at Day 25 for any account below threshold — every Day 30 call happens with good data and expansion proposals go out on time. Health Scorecard in progress may cover part of this — worth aligning on scope.
MediumNo automated D14/D30 lift report — manually prepared before every call
The problem
Every Day 14 and Day 30 data report is manually built per account before each call — pulling metrics, formatting, contextualizing. At 11 accounts this is ~2h/week and grows linearly.
Impact across accounts~2h/week today
~2h/week at 11 accounts. At 30 accounts = 6h/week = one full CSM day just on report prep. Health Scorecard in progress may address this — key question is whether it generates per-account lift summaries at milestone moments.
The unlockPartial: in progress
Automated lift report at D14 and D30. CSM walks into every expansion call with data ready. 6h/week freed at 30 accounts — equivalent to one additional CSM's capacity. Health Scorecard work may cover this — worth aligning on scope.
MediumLift quality monitoring — no flag when lift falls below threshold after 40–50 days
The problem
No automated flag when cumulative lift is low after 40–50 days live. This is caught during manual data prep before calls, meaning we can nearly walk into expansion conversations with weak or misleading data.
Impact across accounts2 confirmed
Airboat Adventures and Ciao Florence: lift below threshold at 40–50 days. Caught during manual review. Algorithm deep dives for HTA confirmed — performance was below expectation and required investigation. All cases were reactive discoveries.
The unlock
Automated flag at day 40 when lift is below threshold → investigation before the operator is disappointed → "here's what we found and what we're doing" rather than a call where the data doesn't look right. Protects every expansion conversation.
Onboarding & expansion
MediumProduct creation — manual ticket step before products appear in the UI feed
The problem
To add a product to Walkway, it doesn't auto-appear in the UI product feed. A ticket must be created for the team to add it — until resolved, setup can't begin and the operator waits.
Impact across accountsEvery batch
Delayed a Bokun account by 1 week. Amigo NYC + Sagrada onboarding: Ventrata product IDs needed to be manually added to UI — confirmed ticket in dataset. At 30-product Amigo batches in July, days of delay directly cost WAAP.
The unlockIn progress
Full ingestion revamp so products auto-populate (Emmanuel, in progress). No ticket queue between "operator commits" and "products appear in the UI." Expansion batches committed and set up in the same week.
MediumOnboarding scalability — process validated at 5–7 products, Q3 needs 15–30 at a time
The problem
The full onboarding process has only been run at 5–7 products per batch. Q3 Scale accounts need 15–30 at a time — and the pipeline has not been tested at that volume.
Impact across accountsNot yet tested
No large-batch onboarding completed. If a 30-product batch breaks mid-onboarding, the operator's confidence in Walkway as a scalable partner is damaged exactly when we're asking for full portfolio commitment.
The unlock
A test run or team confirmation before the Amigo July commitment. Once validated, large-batch expansion becomes a reliable motion — and the full portfolio ask can be made with confidence on both sides.
MediumWinning the star product — no simulation for products not yet activated
The problem
Operators with high-value flagship products price them manually because their own strategy is working. They won't hand these to Walkway without seeing the algorithm match or beat their approach on those specific products.
Impact across accountsBlocks Scale 3a
Full portfolio expansion for our highest-value Scale accounts relies on trust alone today. Positioning presets (in exploration) is a step toward this — letting operators define their strategy before committing crown jewels.
The unlockPartial: in exploration
A back-tested view — "here's what Walkway would have recommended on this product last month." Positioning presets (in exploration) is the right direction. Together these turn the full portfolio ask from a trust conversation into a data decision.
