4 · Two CS motions — new operators vs. mature accounts
New operators follow the standard 30/60/90. Mature accounts — already live and expanding — follow a different motion. The conversation starts where they are, not at onboarding.
Motion A — New operators (just signed)
Day 0 → 7
Contract signed · kickoff call · first push live within 7 days. Gate: 100% autopilot on starting products. If not → PA escalation immediately.
Day 14
First data review call. What moved, why, early competitor signal. CSM-led.
Day 30 ★
Revenue lift confirmed + expansion proposal in same meeting.
"You made $X more this month. Here are the next products with the same pattern."
Day 45
Verbal commit · batch 2 onboarding · live by end of Month 2.
Motion B — Mature accounts (Amigo, Boost, Ciao, Venice)
Phase 2 Start
These accounts are already expanding. Pick up fresh — not an onboarding. Monthly data call to build momentum. Quarterly portfolio review to close the bigger ask.
Monthly (30 min)
Lead with data: revenue premium this month, competitor moves, new opportunities. No hard ask — teach them to see pricing as a revenue lever.
Quarterly (60 min)
3-month cumulative lift. Benchmark vs. similar operators. Full portfolio proposal with ROI math per product.
"Your remaining [N] products match this pattern. Here's what that's worth annually."

Why full portfolio expansion doesn't happen immediately — and what removes each blocker
Product

Onboarding capacity

Max products per day from one account is unconfirmed. Must ask engineering before committing to any large batch.

→ Confirm with engineering before Amigo July batch
Algorithm

No signal on new products

New products have no booking history. Algorithm needs data to make strong recommendations. First batch builds that signal.

→ Show richer data after 30 days as the expansion proof
Trust

Value needs to be demonstrated first

Operators commit their full catalog after seeing the algorithm work — not before. Each batch earns the right to the next ask.

→ Day 30 lift data is the unlock
Cost

Full portfolio = significant commitment

Amigo's full portfolio at $250 = $50K/month. That commitment requires demonstrated ROI per product first.

→ Show ROI per product · offer portfolio commitment promo
Approval

Multi-team sign-off (Amigo)

Each city has its own team and approval chain. Full portfolio = multiple simultaneous approvals. Batching by city is the path.

→ Map champion per city · commit city by city
Internal

PA manual setup per product

PA configures every product manually. Large batches queue behind other accounts and slow onboarding for everyone.

→ Agree max batch size with PA before committing to timeline
