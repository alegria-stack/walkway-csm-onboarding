Trust & reliability
HighActivateAutopilot failure detection
What it is
Algorithm fires recommendations but prices never push to the booking system. No alert is triggered — discovery is reactive.
In practice7 tickets
1 confirmed account (Mi Bike Tours) — autopilot enabled but no prices pushed for 30 days, discovered when the operator flagged it. 7 tickets tracked across related delivery and pickup issues.
Risk at scale
Every active account is exposed. Without proactive detection, silent failures become unexplained churn — and operators blame the product, not a bug.
HighActivatePricing correctness — wrong currency, floor/ceiling errors, pushes operators don't trust
What it is
Operators receive incorrect price outputs — wrong currency, prices stuck at floor or ceiling, pushes too high or too low, algorithm behavior they can't explain.
In practice10 tickets
Largest single issue cluster across the client base. Examples: MXN/USD currency mismatch, algorithm hitting ceiling for 4+ days without moving, mass price revert.
Risk at scale
Pricing errors are the operators evaluate algorithm health primarily through price outputs. When prices look unexpected it prompts a check-in — which is why this area works hand in hand with proactive go-live monitoring and clear visibility into what was pushed.
HighActivateUI visibility — operators can't confirm what Walkway pushed
What it is
Push history is incomplete or stale. Operators see false "Changes Waiting" statuses, grey calendar cells, or no recent activity — even when pushes have fired.
In practice7 tickets
"Did the push actually happen, and can I see it?" is the most common product-trust question across the client base.
Risk at scale
If operators can't see Walkway working in the first 30 days, the Day 30 expansion conversation is built on blind trust, not evidence. Structural blocker to the Proof → Scale transition.
HighActivate + ScaleAutopilot movement tracking — ON→OFF→ON history not captured
What it is
We record when autopilot first goes live — but not when it turns off, comes back on, or how long it was inactive. Full movement history doesn't exist.
In practice1 account · 3 products
Venice City Tours turned off autopilot on 3 products due to a pricing issue. Not detected until the team checked manually — no timestamp or duration captured.
Risk at scale
WAAP can be overstated if products silently drop. Day 30 lift data is unreliable if autopilot was partially off — weakening the expansion proposal at exactly the wrong moment.
HighScaleWinning the star product — no simulation tool for non-activated products
What it is
Operators with high-value flagship products price them manually because their strategy is working. No tool today shows projected Walkway performance on a product not yet activated.
In practiceActive blocker
Full portfolio expansion for the two highest-value Scale accounts depends on operators trusting Walkway with their best products — and today we can't show them what that would look like.
Risk at scale
Without a "what would Walkway have recommended?" view, the full portfolio ask is based on trust alone. This is the unlock for the highest-MRR expansion conversations in Q3.
Scale & CS capacity
HighActivateNo proactive go-live monitoring for new autopilot products
What it is
When a product first goes live, nothing confirms it's pushing correctly in the first 24–72 hours. Today: manual reminders set per account to check in at 24h, then 1 week.
In practiceEvery go-live
Affects every new product activation today. Operators have flagged issues before the team caught them — setting a reactive tone at the most trust-critical moment.
Risk at scale
At 30+ go-lives per quarter, manual reminders will miss cases. Those missed cases are the ones most likely to cause early churn — within the first two weeks of autopilot.
HighActivateNo 30-day at-risk alert — accounts approaching Day 30 with low autopilot go undetected
What it is
No automated flag when an account approaches Day 30 with autopilot below the 90% threshold. The team tracks this manually across all active accounts.
In practiceTime-bomb
No missed cases confirmed yet — manageable at 13 active accounts. This is a dependency that breaks at a specific scale threshold, not gradually.
Risk at scale
At 25 accounts someone slips. At 35, multiple accounts hit Day 30 under threshold undetected. Missed Day 30 = missed expansion proposal = 4-week delay + weakened relationship.
MediumExpandLift quality monitoring — no flag when lift falls below 5% after 40–50 days
What it is
No system flags when cumulative lift is low after 40–50 days live. Caught manually during data prep before the Day 30 call — never proactively.
In practice2 accounts
Airboat Adventures and Ciao Florence both had lift below 5% at 40–50 days. Discovered during manual review — then a ticket was raised for the team to investigate why.
Risk at scale
Low lift discovered late means the expansion conversation is built on weak data. An automated flag at 40 days allows intervention before the operator is already disappointed.
MediumActivate + ScaleIntegration launch stability — no SLA tracking for new integration issues
What it is
New integration launches generate a wave of operator-reported issues in the first 30 days. Each issue requires a manual Linear ticket and follow-up — no structured SLA or intake process.
In practiceBokun pattern
Bokun is the highest-friction integration surface — autopilot pickup failures, catalog scoping, price push visibility. PrioTicket launches Jul 6 and will follow the same pattern.
Risk at scale
At 10+ new Prio operators in July, manual issue intake through the CSM is not sustainable. A structured resolution path protects operator trust in the critical first 30 days.
MediumExpandNo automated D14/D30 lift report — manually prepared before every call
What it is
Before every Day 14 and Day 30 call, the CSM manually pulls lift metrics, formats data, and prepares a per-account performance summary. There is no automated report generation tied to these motion milestones — each report is built from scratch per account.
In practice~2h/week
At 11 active accounts today, approximately 2 hours per week goes into manual report preparation — time that competes directly with demos, expansion proposals, and onboarding calls.
GTM cost if unresolved
At 30 accounts = 6h/week = one full day per CSM per week just on reports. Hard ceiling on how many accounts one CSM can carry without sacrificing call quality or expansion velocity.

MediumActivate + ScaleManual ticket step before products appear in the Walkway UI feed
What it is
Products don't auto-appear in the Walkway UI when added. A ticket must be created for the team to add the product to the feed before setup can begin — adding days per batch.
In practiceEvery batch
Affects every expansion batch today. In progress as a platform improvement but not yet live. Each ticket adds 1–3 days to the start of onboarding.
Risk at scale
At 30-product Amigo batches, this delay compounds. Every day a product isn't in the feed is a day it's not on autopilot — directly costing WAAP.
MediumScaleOnboarding scalability — process validated at 5–7 products, Q3 requires 15–30
What it is
The end-to-end onboarding process — ticket, feed entry, setup, test, go-live — has only been run at batches of 5–7 products. Q3 Scale accounts require batches of 15–30.
In practiceNot yet tested
No large-batch onboarding has been completed. The risk is unknown until attempted — which makes committing to a 30-product July batch without validating the process first a meaningful risk.
Risk at scale
If a large batch fails or delays mid-onboarding, the operator's confidence in Walkway as a scalable partner is damaged exactly when we're asking for full portfolio commitment.
MediumScaleOTA data gap — algorithm only sees direct bookings
What it is
This applies primarily when an operator connects only one booking channel to Walkway (most commonly on Bokun) while also selling through OTAs on a separate platform. When the algorithm only sees one channel, it works from an incomplete demand picture. The opportunity is finding the right optimization approach for operators with a split-channel strategy.
In practice1 account
Venice City Tours: algorithm hurt packs ~2% because OTA bookings were invisible. Operator turned off autopilot on affected products as a result.
Risk at scale
Affects any operator running significant OTA volume alongside direct. Will block expansion for OTA-heavy accounts in the pipeline and is particularly damaging for capacity-dependent products.
MediumReferNo automated case study trigger at milestone events
What it is
No system captures when an operator crosses a meaningful lift milestone and initiates a case study workflow. Case study creation is manually triggered and disconnected from performance data.
In practice0 automated
All case studies to date have been manually initiated. Timing is unpredictable and not tied to when operators actually hit milestones worth documenting.
Risk at scale
8 case studies needed in Q3. Terry's outreach sequences depend on them arriving at the right moment. Without a trigger, case study delivery is unpredictable and outreach can't rely on them.
MediumScaleNo self-serve training materials for operators — every onboarding is manual
What it is
There are no product-embedded tutorials, video guides, or self-serve training assets for operators setting up Walkway. Every onboarding — price rules, ceiling/floor setup, autopilot configuration — is walked through live by the CSM. No reusable materials that operators can reference independently after the call.
In practiceEvery onboarding
Every onboarding session covers the same foundational setup steps from scratch. Operators who need to revisit a configuration step between calls have no self-serve resource — they create a support request or wait for the next scheduled call.
GTM cost if unresolved
Limits how many operators a single CSM can onboard concurrently. Also increases time-to-autopilot when operators get stuck on setup steps post-call. At 30+ operators, self-serve guides directly reduce CSM dependency and accelerate WAAP.
